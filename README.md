## Problem Title
SIH 25006: Development of a Digital Farm Management Portal for implementing Biosecurity measures in Pig and Poultry Farms
## Problem Description
### Background

Biosecurity is a cornerstone of animal health management, particularly in the pig and poultry sectors, where disease outbreaks such as Avian Influenza and African Swine Fever can cause significant economic losses, threaten food security, and disrupt rural livelihoods. Despite its importance, many farmers—especially smallholders in resource-limited areas—struggle to access practical, actionable information on biosecurity protocols, risk assessment tools, and regulatory compliance requirements.

### Problem Description

There is an urgent need for a user-friendly, digital platform that empowers farmers to implement, monitor, and sustain robust biosecurity practices on their farms. This portal should offer end-to-end solutions for farm-level biosecurity management by integrating:

• Customizable risk assessment tools based on local epidemiological conditions.
• Interactive training modules and best practice guidelines tailored for pig and poultry production systems.
• Compliance tracking features aligned with regulatory frameworks to help farmers work toward disease-free compartment recognition.
• Real-time alerts and monitoring dashboards for disease outbreaks and biosecurity breaches.
• Multilingual and mobile-first design to ensure accessibility in remote and rural areas.

The platform should also enable data collection and analysis for policy support, foster collaborative networking among stakeholders (farmers, veterinarians, extension workers, etc.), and promote long-term resilience and sustainability in the livestock sector.

### Expected Outcomes

• Enhanced farmer awareness and education on biosecurity.
• Improved risk management at the farm level as well as self-assessment.
• Easy access to customized biosecurity protocols and guidelines.
• Digital record-keeping and compliance tracking.
• Timely alerts and disease notifications to farmers.
• Healthier livestock and increased farm productivity.
• Empowerment of small and marginal farmers with limited resources.
• Support to authorities in data-driven surveillance and policy making.
• Stronger collaboration across the livestock ecosystem.
• Improved national preparedness for zoonotic and transboundary diseases.

## Proposed Solution
The proposed solution is to develop a Digital Farm Management Portal that will enable pig and poultry farmers to implement, monitor, and maintain effective biosecurity measures. This platform will be a web and mobile-based solution designed to make biosecurity practices easily understandable and practically applicable to all farmers, including small and marginal ones in rural areas. The portal will function as a one-stop digital ecosystem, integrating biosecurity assessment, training, compliance tracking, and real-time disease alert systems.

The Digital Farm Management Portal will allow farmers to assess the level of biosecurity on their farms through a customizable risk assessment tool. This tool will collect farm-related data such as hygiene levels, feed storage, animal movement, and visitor control measures, and then generate a biosecurity risk score based on artificial intelligence algorithms. Farmers will receive instant feedback and personalized recommendations for improvement. Alongside, the portal will include an interactive training and knowledge center that provides multimedia lessons, audio-visual demonstrations, and multilingual support. This ensures that farmers with low literacy can also understand and implement biosecurity standards efficiently.

The system will also include features for compliance tracking and digital record management. Farmers can maintain all their farm records, including vaccination logs, disinfection schedules, and inspection details, in one secure online location. The portal will automatically generate compliance reports in line with government regulations, making it easier for farmers to qualify for disease-free certifications. Moreover, the system will send timely alerts and reminders regarding upcoming tasks, inspections, and potential disease outbreaks in nearby areas.

To support quick communication and collaboration, the portal will include built-in networking tools where farmers can contact veterinarians, agricultural officers, and other farmers for guidance or consultation. The platform will be mobile-first, ensuring that it runs efficiently on smartphones, and will include offline accessibility for areas with poor internet connectivity. Through this, even farmers in remote locations will be able to use the application effectively.

This proposed solution directly addresses the challenges of limited awareness, low accessibility to reliable biosecurity information, and weak disease management systems. It empowers farmers by giving them practical tools to protect livestock health, thereby reducing disease outbreaks, minimizing economic losses, and improving productivity.

The solution uniquely integrates AI, IoT, GIS, and multilingual voice assistance to deliver smart, accessible, and data-driven biosecurity management.

## Technical Approach
The technical development of this portal will be based on a combination of modern, scalable, and secure technologies. The frontend will be developed using React.js for web and Flutter for mobile applications to ensure cross-platform compatibility. The backend will be built using either Node.js with Express.js or Django REST Framework for managing data and APIs. A database such as PostgreSQL or MongoDB will be used for secure and structured data storage. Artificial Intelligence components will be implemented using Python libraries like Pandas, NumPy, and Scikit-learn, while mapping and visualization features will rely on GIS tools and Google Maps APIs.

The portal will also integrate cloud-based services such as AWS or Azure for hosting and storage, ensuring reliability and scalability. For multilingual support, APIs such as Google Translate or India’s Bhashini platform will be used to provide translation and voice assistance in regional languages. The methodology for implementation will begin with requirement analysis by engaging with farmers, veterinarians, and government agencies to understand their needs. After this, the design phase will involve creating user-friendly UI/UX mockups in Figma, followed by coding, integration, and rigorous testing for performance, security, and usability. Finally, the system will be deployed on cloud servers with continuous updates and maintenance.

The workflow of the system will begin when the user logs into the platform and provides farm details. The AI engine will then perform a risk assessment and generate a biosecurity score. Based on this, the system will provide recommendations, training content, and alerts. The user can track compliance, generate reports, and stay informed about local disease outbreaks through real-time notifications.

## Feasibility and Viability
The idea is highly feasible as it is based on existing, cost-effective, and open-source technologies that can be customized according to the project’s requirements. From a technical standpoint, the architecture is scalable and can handle large amounts of data securely. Operationally, it is feasible since most rural farmers now have access to smartphones and basic internet connectivity. The mobile-first design ensures usability even on low-end devices. Economically, it is viable as it can be developed with minimal hardware investment and can receive funding support from government programs or agricultural innovation schemes.

However, some challenges may arise during implementation. The major ones include limited digital literacy among rural farmers, inconsistent internet connectivity, and concerns about data privacy. These challenges can be overcome by including voice-based features, offline access modes, and strong encryption for data protection. Additionally, awareness programs and training sessions can be conducted to encourage farmers to adopt the digital platform confidently.

## Impact and Benefits
The proposed portal will have a profound impact on the pig and poultry farming community as well as on national biosecurity efforts. It will empower farmers with knowledge and digital tools to prevent disease outbreaks and improve the overall health of livestock. By promoting proper hygiene and farm management, it will help reduce mortality rates, increase productivity, and enhance income levels for small and marginal farmers. Socially, it will foster collaboration between farmers, veterinarians, and local authorities, promoting community-level disease control. Economically, it will prevent financial losses caused by disease epidemics and improve market access for healthy livestock. Environmentally, the adoption of biosecure and sustainable practices will lead to cleaner and safer farming systems. From a government perspective, the data collected will support evidence-based policymaking and strengthen national preparedness for zoonotic and transboundary diseases.
